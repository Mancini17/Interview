Interview With Ms.Ross

Interview by Matthew Mancini

MANCINI - Why do you want to be a designer?

MS. ROSS - I want to be a graphic designer because I want to used my creative skills in my everyday life. I love the idea of working with different kinds of clients and also think that being a graphic designer can provide me with everyday problems and challenges that I will enjoy solving.

MANCINI - What makes you nervous about becoming a designer?

MS. ROSS - What makes me nervous is that graphic design is a very broad term. There are a lot of different fields and directions of which I can enter, and as of now I am not 100% positive where I will end up in the field.

MANCINI - What excites you about design?

MS. ROSS - What excited me about design is that it is limitless. It is continuously evolving. I also love that it can communicate in so many different ways.

MANCINI - What are your strengths?

MS. ROSS - I think my strengths are my analog work. I am a good illustrator.

MANCINI - What are some areas of opportunity to work on?

MS. ROSS - Some areas that I need to work on are my technical skills in many of the adobe programs. Though I have gotten much better, I still find that there are times when I have a good idea, but simply am limited by my skills in these programs.

MANCINI - What aspects of graphic design do you gravitate towards?

MS. ROSS - I gravitate towards illustration. I am also finding that I really like information graphics.

MANCINI - What are the roles and responsibilities of a graphic designer in society?

MS. ROSS - In my opinion a graphic designer has a very large roll in society. Graphic Designers control almost everything we see and therefore can influence the opinion and attitudes of our consumers. In my opinion society as a whole can be shaped and formed through graphic designers.

MANCINI - When/where/why are you at your most creative/productive?

MS. ROSS - I am at my most creative in the morning, after I have done a workout. I am most productive when I am out of the house and at school or a coffee shop to do work. This is because I have the most energy in the morning after I have worked out. My home for me is my place of rest, and I try not to do any work at home. Instead I get out of the house and work elsewhere.

MANCINI - What are your comfort levels with giving and receiving feedback?

MS. ROSS - I think that I could be better at giving feedback. Sometimes I worry about hurting peoples feelings and saying too much. I love constructive criticism and think it is the only way you will ever get better at what you are doing. I like to receive feedback.

MANCINI - What is a webiste?

MS. ROSS - A website for me is a place on the internet where people can visually and physically interact and receive information and give information.

MANCINI - Why did you choose to come into this program?

MS. ROSS - I chose to come into this program because I was no longer as passionate about the field I previously studied in. I know that I love to be creative and problem solve and this program was a perfect fit for me and where I am at in my life.

MANCINI - Who do you look up to in Graphic Design (inspiration)?

MS. ROSS - I would not say that I have one person in particular that I look up to. Rather I love to be inspired by other peoples work. I often surf through work on Behance and other Internet platforms and become inspired.

MANCINI - So far, which course has been your favourite?

MS. ROSS - So Far my favourite course has been intro to technology with Chris during our first semester.

MANCINI - What are some things that you dislike doing within the program?

MS. ROSS - Some things that I dislike doing in this program are procrastinating. It is really hard to catch up in classes once you are behind, as the work can be very time consuming.
